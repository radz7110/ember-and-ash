How to play

Ember & Ash · full rules
1

You're a clan of one cat. Your cat and your opponent's cat are fighting over five territories on the map below. Each round you have 10 embers (your cat's strength) to secretly split across those five zones however you like — all on one, spread evenly, whatever you think will win.

2

The Ash Throne is worth double. It sits at the center of the map and scores 2 points instead of 1 — everyone can see it's the prize zone, so expect your opponent to contest it hard too.

3

Reveal together. Once you've both locked in your allocation, everything flips face-up at the same moment. In each zone, whoever placed more embers wins it and their cat plants a flag there for the round. Equal embers in a zone means nobody scores it.

4

Falling behind earns you a sabotage card. Whoever has fewer points after a round gets a random sabotage card added to their hand (capped at 3 in hand at once). Cards are also handed out to both players automatically every other round, win or lose.

5

Play at most one sabotage card per round, alongside your normal allocation. Once played, it's gone — so timing matters. Save it for the Ash Throne round, or use it early to shape how your opponent plays.

6

Six rounds total. After round six resolves, whoever has the most zone-points wins the game. Equal scores end in a draw.

The five territories
⛰️The Ridge — 1 point, a steep highland at the map's edge.
🌾The Hollow — 1 point, a low sheltered valley.
🌉The Bridge — 1 point, the crossing that connects everything.
🏺The Market — 1 point, a cluster of trading stalls.
🔥Ash Throne — 2 points, the contested seat of power.
Sabotage cards
A worked example round
Say you have 10 embers. You put 5 on the Ash Throne, 3 on the Bridge, and 2 on the Market, leaving the Ridge and Hollow empty. Your opponent, not realizing you'd commit so hard to the Throne, spreads out: 2 on each of the five zones. At reveal: you win the Throne (5 > 2, worth 2 points) and the Bridge (3 > 2) and the Market (2 = 2, a tie — nobody scores it). Your opponent picks up the Ridge and Hollow, where you placed nothing. Final tally for the round: you score 3 points, your opponent scores 2. Overcommitting to the Throne paid off — but if they'd guessed your plan and stacked the Throne too, you'd have lost your biggest bet.
Playing together
A

Same device: pass the phone or laptop when prompted — the screen between turns keeps the other player from peeking at your allocation.

B

Two devices: one player creates a room and shares the 5-letter code; the other joins with it. Moves sync automatically in the background — just keep both tabs open until the game ends.
